Cookware

There are many different types of cookware on the market. They can fulfil several different purposes or just one. The trick is to evaluate how often you use certain methods of cooking and gradually build up your cookware to accomodate for such.

Beginner's Checklist

Frying Pan

A necessity, you will use this to brown a vast array of foods.

Sauce Pan

Another essential used for soups, sauces and short pasta.

Large Pot

This is vital for cooking in batches which is cost effective.

Roasting Tray

As the name implies, this would be used for roasting.

Baking Tray

This is intended for baking, but can also be used as a roasting tray.

Casserole Dish

Best made from enamaled cast-iron. This is great for one-pot meals.

Pots and Pans

Pots and pans are the cookware you are likely to use most frequently. They are used for boiling pasta, searing, sautaéing and making sauces. It is therefore important that you get the right ones.

Materials

Choosing the right material might seem difficult at first, but can be made easy. All you have to do is evaluate the kind of food you cook most and your budget. You can then base your decision on those two factors.

Non-stick

£30-£50
Best for: Eggs, delicate foods
Not good for: Searing, sautèing

Stainless Steel

£60-£100
Best for: Searing, frying, sautéing, deglazing
Not good for: Eggs, delicate foods

Cast-Iron

£30-£50
Best for: Searing, baking, stove to oven
Not good for: Large amounts of acidic food
