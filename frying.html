Frying

There are several methods of frying, all of which achieve different results. To some it can be a bit daunting due to fear of hot oil. However, with a little confidence and know how, you have nothing to fear.

Pan Frying

The most common method of frying. It is used to cook larger or whole pieces of food in a small amount of oil. This is usually done around medium-high heat, to ensure that the outside of the food is not burned before the interior fully cooks.

When meat is fried, it often leaves browned residue on the bottom of the pan. This can be deglazed with liquid to make a sauce.

Deep Frying

When food is fully submerged in hot oil, it is being deep fried. While best done with a deep fryer, a large pot is good as well. If using a pot, you will need tongs or a slotted spoon to extract the food and an instant read thermometer to check the oil temperature.

Shallow frying is almost the exact same with the food only being half submerged, meaning it has to be flipped half way through.

Sautéing

Sautéing is used to cook smaller pieces of food. A high heat is necessary to quickly cook the food through and reduce moisture loss.

Stir frying is a similar method, the main difference being that it is typically done in wok and the sauce is made with the food as it cooks.

With both methods, it is important to move the food around to prevent burning. This can be done with a utensil or by tossing.

Tossing Food in a Pan

You will have most likely seen this technique being used on TV. It is not an essential technique per say, however, it is faster and requires less washing up (if the food does not go everywhere( Most importantly though, it will make you look cool.
