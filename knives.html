Knives

The first question you are probably asking is 'what knives do I need?' Luckily the answer to that is straightforward. For home cooking the general consensus is that these knives are essential:

Chef Knife

Blade: 8 inches
Use: Almost anything

Paring Knife

Blade: 4 inches
Use: Peeling, small work

Serrated Knife

Blade: 8 inches
Use: Slicing bread

What makes a good knife?

It does not end there though. There are certain qualities that you should consider when buying a knife, to ensure that you get one that suits you.

A full tang and bolster are ideal as they both provide balance and thus control. Forged knives tend to be higher in quality but are expensive. Therefore, stamped knives are better for beginners as they are cheaper and come in sets. Most importantly though, the knife should be comfortable in your hand.

Use a Cutting Board!

Before you use a knife you need a cutting board. Trying to cut on any other surface will damage your knife. Your choice on what material is either wood or plastic. Others like glass are no good and will blunt your knife more quickly.

Wood

● A well cared board will last a lifetime

● It is the more sanitary option, as bacteria will sink below the surface where they cannot multiply and die

● Regardless, clean after each use by wiping the surface with hot soapy water and dry immediately

● NEVER leave it to soak or it will split and warp

● Care for the board by wiping it with food safe oil every so often

Plastic

● The cheaper option

● Easy to clean and dishwasher safe (in most cases)

● More prone to grooves and ridges being created by knives.

● These will house bacteria and are very difficult to clean.

● With plastic boards, have seperate boards for meat and vegetables.
