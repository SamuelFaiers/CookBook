Knife Skills

Knife skills are possibly the most important techniques one needs to know when cooking. Despite this, many people still do not even know how to hold a knife properly! So to start:

Holding a Knife

The Pinch

By pinching the base of the blade, you maximise the control you have over the knife.

Basics of Cutting

With that, you are now ready to learn the basics of cutting!

Of all the things covered in the above video, the most important how you position your guiding hand. By having your fingers curled back and your thumb tucked behind, you eliminate the risk of losing your favourite digits!

To minimise the risk of mutilation even further, your knife must be sharp, at all times. The easiest way to achieve this is by honing your knife.

Honing your Knife

Do not confuse this with sharpening. When you sharpen a blade, you are removing material to restore the blades sharpness. With honing, you are re-aligning its edge to its best possible angle for cutting.

You should aim to hone your knife once on each side every time before cutting. Note that there will come a time when you will have to properly sharpen your knife, for which you will need a knife sharpener or whetstone.
