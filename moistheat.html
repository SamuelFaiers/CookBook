Moist-heat Cooking

Moist-heat cooking encompasses all methods of cooking that use water or other liquids to cook food. These methods are typically healthier than dry-heat methods as they do not require oil or fat to cook in.

Boiling

Temperature: 100°C.

Large bubbles should rapidly roll up to the surface of the liquid and displace it. Some nutrients in vegetables dissolve in boiling water and thus their flavour. So avoid boiling vegetables if you can.

Best for: Pasta

Simmering

Temperature: 85°C.

In this case there should be fewer bubbles&46; They will rise more gently just breaking the surface of the liquid which remains relatively calm.

Best for: Reducing sauces, soups, stocks and stews

Poaching

Temperature: 71°C.

A very gental simmer. Ideally there should be no bubble, but if there are any they should just sit on the bottom with the occassional bubble rising up.

Best for: Poached eggs and other delicate foods

Steaming

Temperature: 100°C.

Here food placed in a container situated above boiling liquid. The container allows steam to pass through small holes to cook the food and a lid prevents the steam from escaping.

Best for: Vegetables, fish and dumplings
